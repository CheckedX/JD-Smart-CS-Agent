A story about Lila

Twelve-year-old Lila, who has always loved exploring the lush forest near her small town, noticed a terrible change on her weekend visit. Where bright wildflowers once bloomed and clear streams gurgled, she now saw piles of discarded trash under wilted yellowing trees, rough stumps where tall oaks used to stand, and murky, smelly water when she cupped her hands in the stream.

Lila hurried to tell her neighbors the forest was sick, but they just waved her away, saying kids shouldn't meddle and trash there didn't affect their lives. Soon the town's tap water smelled strange, and the teacher explained the forest damage had polluted the water source. Lila made up her mind she would save the forest all by herself if no one would help.

With her careful comparison drawings of the old healthy forest and the current damaged one, plus a clear environmental protection plan, Lila bravely handed her work to the community office. She then found her classmates, held up her hand-drawn posters, and explained that picking up trash and planting new saplings would bring the forest back. Soon, several children decided to join her action team.
