A girl protects the environment and saves the world.

A story about Lila

Lila loves walking in the old forest behind her home, where she feeds nuts to fluffy squirrels and watches silver fish dart in the clear stream. But lately, tall stumps replace the oak trees, yellow dust blows into the town, covering her freshly washed coat, making her grandma cough too hard to go to the market, and her neighbors shake their heads saying ordinary people can change nothing.

Staring at the gray, smog-covered sky outside the classroom window, Lila made a firm decision to take action instead of waiting helplessly. She gathered her closest classmates, and they huddled around wooden desks, dipping their bright watercolor pens in paint to draw vivid posters of green forests and happy wild animals. They wrote the slogan "Protecting forests is protecting our home" on every page, and carefully stapled the pages together into lovely, colorful pamphlets they planned to hand out to the whole neighborhood.

On a sunny weekend, Lila set up a small table with her friends in the community square. She shared stories of forest animals losing their homes and showed her before-and-after photos of the local woods. Many neighbors were surprised to learn how environmental changes connect to everyone's daily lives, and dozens of them signed her forest protection initiative.
