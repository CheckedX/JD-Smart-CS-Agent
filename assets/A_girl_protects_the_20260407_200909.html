A girl protects the environment and saves the world.

A story about Luna

On a bright Saturday morning, Luna carried her wicker basket to the nearby forest to pick wildflowers, just as she did every weekend. But as soon as she stepped through the forest edge, she noticed something was terribly wrong: the clear stream she loved to dip her feet in was now murky brown, pieces of strange industrial trash littered the soft green grass, the fluffy squirrel that usually chattered and waved at her was huddled in a tree hole coughing weakly, and the tall oak trees were dropping yellow, crispy leaves even though it was still mid-summer.

Key Words: environment, pollution

Luna carefully carried the weak, sneezing baby squirrel to the local animal rescue center, her chest tight with worry. The kind staff told her that industrial waste had been illegally dumped in the nearby forest, poisoning plants and making dozens of wild animals sick. They said the clean-up work was progressing far too slowly, and if nothing changed soon, the pollution would spread to the whole town. Luna squeezed her fists tightly, her eyes shining with determination: she would do whatever it took to protect the forest she loved.

Wearing thick work gloves and carrying her notebook, Luna walked carefully through the forest, taking photos of different types of trash and marking every polluted spot on her map. After collecting all the evidence she needed, she went back to school, gathered her closest friends, and they worked together to draw fun comics, look up environmental facts, make colourful information brochures, and practice a short play about protecting nature.
